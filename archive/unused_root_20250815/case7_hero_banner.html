Engineer A, an experienced environmental engineer, considers AI tools to draft report text and assist design documents after losing a mentor’s review support. Key tensions: confidentiality, responsible charge, competence, and attribution.

Decision points (at a glance)
DP1 · AI-generated report text Confidentiality risk
Ethical if: competence and thorough review are met; unethical if client data is exposed or attribution omitted.
Principle: Honesty/Transparency Obligation: Client Confidentiality (II.1.c) Obligation: Professional Credit (III.9)
DP2 · AI-assisted design documents Responsible charge
Unethical if only cursory review is performed before sealing; must retain personal direction and comprehensive verification.
Obligation: Responsible Charge (II.2.b) Principle: Public Welfare (I.1) Action: Detailed Technical Review
DP3 · Disclosure of AI use Contract-governed
No universal obligation to disclose; voluntary transparency may build trust and clarify attribution.
Principle: Avoid Deception (I.5) Principle: Truthfulness (III.3) Resource: Contract Terms
