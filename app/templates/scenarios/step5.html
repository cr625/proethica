About Step 5 Architecture

Purpose: Step 5 visualizes Step 4 analysis in scenario format to reveal ethical problem structure more clearly than narrative text.

Stages 1-6 (Implemented):
  • Stage 1: Data Collection - Gathers all entities from Passes 1-3
  • Stage 2: Timeline Construction - Builds chronological event sequence
  • Stage 3: Participant Mapping - Creates character profiles with LLM enhancement
  • Stage 4: Decision Identification - References Step 4 Part D (institutional analysis)
  • Stage 5: Causal Chains - References Step 4 Part E (action-rule mapping)
  • Stage 6: Normative Framework - References Step 4 Part F (transformation)
Stages 7-9 (Planned):
  • Stage 7: Scenario Assembly - Combine all components
  • Stage 8: Interactive Model - Generate decision points
  • Stage 9: Validation & QA - Quality checks
Key Design: Step 4 performs comprehensive analysis, Step 5 visualizes it in scenario format. This architectural separation enables precise precedent discovery and cross-case pattern analysis.
