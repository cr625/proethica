What are Professional Domains?

Professional domains represent specialized fields of practice where ethical decisions are made. They contain roles, principles, obligations, and other entities that form the framework for ethical scenarios.

Each domain is based on an ontology that defines the types of entities and their relationships. Domains provide the field-specific elements, rules, and constraints that shape the ethical decision-making process in professional scenarios.

When you create a domain, you establish the foundation for multiple scenarios that can explore different ethical challenges within that professional field. The domain defines what types of roles, resources, and conditions are available, while scenarios represent specific situations within that domain.
