Study reference
What happens in each case

Each case proceeds through four steps. The pills at the top of the case page mark the current position.

  1. Facts. Read the case Facts. On the Facts screen, a “Questions before the Board” card summarizes what the Board was asked to decide. The Board’s full Discussion is presented at the Wrap-up step. The Conclusions view in step 2 pairs each question with its bottom-line ruling; the underlying reasoning and cited code provisions are reserved for the Wrap-up.
  2. Views. Examine the five views and rate each on a 1–7 scale (about 15 items per case, three per view). Detailed recall of every entry is not required. The rating evaluates how well each view supports a reader’s understanding.
  3. Reflection. Three short rating items about the views as a whole, followed by two open-ended prompts. The ratings are required.
  4. Wrap-up. The Board’s Discussion and Conclusions are revealed for context, followed by one final reflection.
The five views
Narrative
The people involved, what is at stake, and the ethical tensions their roles produce.
Timeline
The sequence of actions and events, with the points where ethical obligations became active.
Conclusions
The Board’s rulings on each question it addressed, with related analytical questions surfaced for additional perspective.
Decisions
The choices the professional faced, with the arguments for and against each option.
Provisions
Which sections of the NSPE Code of Ethics apply to the case, and how they connect to specific facts.
After your cases

After your final case, one short page remains. You rank the five views from most to least useful for understanding cases overall, answer one yes/no question about whether the views surfaced anything you would have missed, and add any open comments.
