Before you start

A short walk-through of what happens in the study.

Your participant code is {{ participant_code }} . You can pause and resume by re-entering this code at any time.

1What happens in each case

Each case proceeds through four steps. The pills at the top of the case page mark the current position.

  1. Facts. Read the case Facts. On the Facts screen, a “Questions before the Board” card summarizes what the Board was asked to decide. The Board’s full Discussion is presented at the Wrap-up step (step 4). The Conclusions view in step 2 pairs each Board question with its bottom-line ruling. The underlying reasoning, cited code provisions, and historical BER case references are reserved for the Wrap-up.
  2. Views. Examine the five views described in section 2 below and rate each on a 1–7 scale (about 15 items per case, three per view). Detailed recall of every entry is not required. The rating evaluates how well each view supports a reader’s understanding.
  3. Reflection. Three short rating items about the five views as a whole, followed by two open-ended prompts. The ratings are required.
  4. Wrap-up. The Board’s Discussion and Conclusions for the case are revealed for context, followed by one final reflection.

2The five views of the case

Each view shows the same case from a different angle, surfacing different aspects of what happened. You move through them in tab order, with the sequence going from the case as a human story to the formal code provisions that ground it.

Narrative
The people involved, what is at stake, and the ethical tensions their roles produce.
Timeline
The sequence of actions and events, with the points where ethical obligations became active.
Conclusions
The Board’s rulings on each question it addressed, with related analytical questions surfaced for additional perspective.
Decisions
The choices the professional faced, with the arguments for and against each option.
Provisions
Which sections of the NSPE Code of Ethics apply to the case, and how they connect to specific facts.

3After your cases

After your final case, one short page remains. You rank the five views from most to least useful for understanding cases overall, answer one yes/no question about whether the views surfaced anything you would have missed in the facts alone, and add any open comments.
