Before you start

A short walk-through of what happens in the study.

Your participant code is {{ participant_code }}. Write it down or screenshot this page. You can pause and resume by re-entering this code at any time.

1What happens in each case

  1. Read the case facts. The board's Discussion and Conclusions are hidden at first so you can form your own understanding before reading theirs.
  2. Look at five views of the case. Each view organizes the same case from a different angle. You don't need to read every entry. Form an impression of which view's framing helped you most.
  3. Rate each view on a 1–7 scale, from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree. About 18 short items per case.
  4. Answer four short open-ended questions about the case in your own words.
  5. The board's Discussion and Conclusions are revealed for you to read.
  6. Rate how well your earlier answers lined up with the board's reasoning.

2The five views of the case

Each view shows the same case from a different angle, surfacing different aspects of what happened. You move through them in tab order, with the sequence going from the case as a human story to the formal code provisions that ground it.

Narrative
The people involved, what is at stake, and the ethical tensions their roles produce.
Timeline
The sequence of actions and events, with the points where ethical obligations became active.
Q&C
Questions the board asked about the case, paired with how each was answered.
Decisions
The choices the professional faced, with the arguments for and against each option.
Provisions
Which sections of the NSPE Code of Ethics apply to the case, and how they connect to specific facts.
