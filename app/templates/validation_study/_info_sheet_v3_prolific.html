Title: Evaluating Structured Synthesis for Professional Ethics Case Analysis Using the ProEthica System

IRB Study #: 2603011709

Investigators:
Rosina O. Weber, PhD (Principal Investigator) · rw37@drexel.edu
Christopher B. Rauch (Student Investigator) · cr625@drexel.edu
College of Computing and Informatics, Drexel University

You are being asked to participate in a research study. Taking part in this research is voluntary. Whether you take part is up to you. You may withdraw at any time, and it will not be held against you. Deciding not to be in the research study, now or later, will not impact your relationship with Drexel University or with Prolific.

Eligibility

This study is open to adults 18 years of age or older who have engineering education (current student or graduate with an engineering degree) or who currently work in an engineering occupation. Eligibility was confirmed by Prolific's pre-screening filters before this invitation was sent.

Purpose

This research investigates whether a structured case analysis tool called ProEthica helps people understand professional ethics cases. ProEthica organizes information from ethics cases into multiple analytical views, and this study evaluates five of them: Provisions, Questions and Conclusions, Decisions, Timeline, and Narrative. We want to know whether these views are useful for understanding the ethical issues in a case.

What you will be asked to do

You will review 3–4 published ethics cases from the National Society of Professional Engineers (NSPE) Board of Ethical Review. For each case, you will read the case facts, review five ProEthica analysis views, rate each view on a short questionnaire, and answer four open-ended questions about the case. After completing all assigned cases, you will answer a brief set of reflection questions about the overall experience and a short demographic questionnaire. You can choose to skip any question you do not wish to answer.

Time commitment

Your participation will take approximately 45–60 minutes. Your progress is saved automatically; you may pause and resume using your participant code if needed.

Compensation

You will be paid $9.00 USD via Prolific upon successful completion of the study. A unique completion code will be displayed on the final page of the study; you must paste this code into Prolific within 14 days for payment to be processed. Prolific's standard payment timing applies. Partial completion is not compensated.

Risks

The risks of participating in this study are minimal. You may experience minor fatigue from the research activities. The cases you will review are published professional ethics opinions that are publicly available. Although steps are taken to protect your research information, there is always the potential risk of information being shared unintentionally.

Benefits

You may benefit from structured exposure to NSPE ethics cases and from reflecting on how different presentation formats affect ethical analysis. There are no other direct benefits beyond the listed compensation.

Confidentiality

We may publish the results of this research. However, no identifying information will be linked to your responses. You are assigned a random participant code on enrollment, and all study data is stored under this code only. Your Prolific Participant ID is used only to confirm study completion to Prolific so that payment can be processed; the Prolific PID is not retained in study data after payment. We will not share your research information for use in future research with your identifiable information.

Voluntary nature of participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without consequence beyond forfeiture of compensation for incomplete participation. The research team has no relationship to your Prolific account standing; declining to participate or withdrawing will not affect your Prolific reputation.

Questions and concerns

If you have questions about the study, please contact the research team using Prolific's messaging system or at the email addresses provided above. This research is being overseen by an Institutional Review Board (IRB). An IRB is a group of people who perform independent review of research studies to make sure the rights and welfare of participants are protected. You may speak to the Drexel IRB at (267) 359-2471 or HRPP@drexel.edu if you have questions about your rights as a research participant.

This study is reviewed under Drexel IRB Protocol 2603011709.
