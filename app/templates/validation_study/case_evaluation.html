View 1 of 5: Narrative
Roles, and the obligations they carry, create the tensions. Each person on this view holds a professional role. The obligations attached to those roles are grounded in the NSPE Code of Ethics, and where the obligations of different roles pull against each other, an ethical tension arises. Read the characters first, then the tensions they produce.
Narrative View
Characters and ethical tensions, presented with an opening context. The case’s ethical questions arise from the responsibilities each role carries. An ethical tension appears when two obligations both have a legitimate claim on the professional, and honoring one would mean falling short on the other. This view begins with the people involved and what is at stake.

View 4 of 5: Decisions
At each decision point the professional could have chosen differently. This view shows the options considered, the argument structure for and against each, and the Board's chosen option. Click any decision to read its full detail.
Decisions View
A decision point is a moment at which the professional could have chosen otherwise. Each decision card shows the full question, the options considered (with the Board’s choice marked), and the argument structure for and against the choice. The argument structure includes warrants that support the choice, rebuttals that challenge it, and grounds drawn from the case facts. Together these give a future case reviewer alternatives to weigh and reasoning to test when applying this case as precedent.
