Information Sheet for Participation in Research

Title: Evaluating Structured Synthesis for Professional Ethics Case Analysis Using the ProEthica System

IRB Study #: 2603011709

Investigators:
Rosina O. Weber, PhD (Principal Investigator) — rw37@drexel.edu
Christopher B. Rauch (Student Investigator) — cr625@drexel.edu
College of Computing and Informatics, Drexel University

You are being asked to participate in a research study. Taking part in this research is voluntary. Whether you take part is up to you, you may withdraw your participation at any time, and it will not be held against you. Deciding not to be in the research study, now or later, will not impact your relationship with Drexel University.

Purpose

This research investigates whether a structured case analysis tool called ProEthica helps people understand professional ethics cases. ProEthica organizes information from ethics cases into multiple analytical views, and this study evaluates five of them: Provisions, Questions and Conclusions, Decisions, Timeline, and Narrative. We want to know whether these views are useful for understanding the ethical issues in a case.

What you will be asked to do

You will review 3–4 published ethics cases from the National Society of Professional Engineers (NSPE) Board of Ethical Review. For each case, you will read the case facts, review five ProEthica analysis views, rate each view on a short questionnaire, and answer four open-ended questions about the case. After completing all assigned cases, you will answer a brief set of reflection questions about the overall experience. You can choose to not answer any question you do not wish to answer.

Time commitment

Your participation will take approximately 1 hour total. You may complete the research activities across multiple sessions within a two-week window. Your progress is saved automatically.

Risks

The risks of participating in this study are minimal. You may experience minor fatigue from the research activities. The cases you will review are published professional ethics opinions that are publicly available. Although steps will be taken to protect your research information, there is always the potential risk of your information being shared unintentionally.

Benefits

You may benefit from structured exposure to NSPE ethics cases and from reflecting on how different presentation formats affect ethical analysis.

Confidentiality

We may publish the results of this research. However, no identifying information will be collected or linked to your responses. You will be assigned a random participant ID, and all data will be stored using this ID only. No names or email addresses will be associated with your responses. We will not share your research information to be used for future research with your identifiable information.

Voluntary nature of participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. The research team has no instructional, grading, or supervisory role in your senior design course. Your course instructors will not be informed whether or not you participate. Your decision to participate or not participate will have no effect on your course grades or academic standing.

Questions and concerns

If you have questions about the study, please contact the research team at the contact information provided above. This research is being overseen by an Institutional Review Board (IRB). An IRB is a group of people who perform independent review of research studies to make sure the rights and welfare of participants are protected. You may speak to the Drexel IRB at (267) 359-2471 or HRPP@drexel.edu if you have questions about your rights as a research participant.

This project was determined to be exempt from federal human subjects research regulations.
